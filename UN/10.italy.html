Invisible Sculpture Sold For ~RM21,319

source: https://thecrestng.com/strange-man-sells-invisible-sculpture-for-n7389000/

When it comes to modern art, some might say that it is ridiculous. But what more absurd is an art that cannot be seen. In Italy, a artist named Salvatore Garau sold an invisible artwork titled "Io Sono" (Italian for "I am"). The sculpture initial price was 6k to 9k euro, but was then raised after a few bids.


The artist argues that his work isn't 'nothing', but is a 'vacuum'. "The vacuum is nothing more than a space full of energy, and even if we empty it and there is nothing left, according to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle, that 'nothing' has a weight," he claims.
